Understanding the structure of music

In music, while it may seem very easy to feel and interpret musical ideas, it can become very difficult when entering the theoretical aspect of music.

In general, musicians should be able to understand the notes and the relationship between them. There are a total of 12 different notes that are repeated at higher frequencies (for example, the C key on a piano is repeated more than one time, and all sound higher or lower, depending on how high or low you go on the keyboard.

Here is an example path a musician may take in terms of understanding music and improving their musical knowledge:
  1. Notes (where the notes are on your particular instrument)
  2. Scales and chords (how songs are written in certain keys)
  3. Intervalls and ear training (understanding the distance between notes and perceiving them by sound)
  4. Advanced chords and modes (advanced theory explores the feelings you can invoke with certain notes and how they interact between each other in a song)

While this is more of a classical approach, some people may pick a more loose way of learning. This is the case for people who learn "by ear". While most people who play for a long time end up studying theory at some point to improve their knowledge, people who learn by ear get various advantages and disadvantages compared to classically trained musicians. There is no clear winner, just pick a method that speaks to you!

Are you someone who likes structure? Or would you rather be left to experiment with trial and error?
Do you want to be able to improvise many songs? Or do you value hard work on a single song, to be able to play difficult songs?
If you want to learn more about musical theory, Youtube is a great ressource! Look around, there is a ton of information on the internet, and it all comes with experience : keep practicing and keep improving!
